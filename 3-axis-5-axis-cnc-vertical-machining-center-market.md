<p><p>In the CNC Vertical Machining Center market, there are three main types based on the number of axes they operate on - 3 axis, 4 axis, and 5 axis machines. A 3 axis machine can move along the X, Y, and Z axes, enabling it to perform basic milling operations. A 4 axis machine adds a rotary axis (usually A or B) to enable the machining of complex curved surfaces. A 5 axis machine adds two additional rotary axes (usually A and B) to allow for even more versatile machining, including the production of intricate and highly detailed parts.</p></p>
