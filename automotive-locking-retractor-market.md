Manual locking retractors require the user to manually lock the seatbelt into place by pulling the webbing. On the other hand, automatic locking retractors automatically lock the seatbelt in place when there is a sudden deceleration or during emergency situations. Manual locking retractors are commonly used in older vehicles, while automatic locking retractors are found in most modern cars for enhanced safety.</p></p>
