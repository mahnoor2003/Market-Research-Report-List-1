<p><p>The automotive sound system market can be categorized into two main types: OEM and Aftermarket. OEM, or Original Equipment Manufacturer, refers to sound systems that come pre-installed in vehicles during the manufacturing process. These systems are designed and supplied by the vehicle manufacturer themselves. On the other hand, Aftermarket sound systems are purchased separately and installed in the vehicle by the vehicle owner or a third-party provider after the vehicle has been manufactured. Aftermarket systems offer customization options and allow for upgrading the sound quality beyond the OEM standard.</p></p>
