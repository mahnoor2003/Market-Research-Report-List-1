<p><p>The bacterial nanocellulose market includes two main types: nanofibrillated cellulose (NFC) and nanocrystalline cellulose (NCC). NFC is cellulose that has been mechanically disintegrated into nanoscale fibrils, possessing high aspect ratio and surface area. It finds applications in composites, paper coatings, and food packaging due to its unique mechanical and barrier properties. NCC, on the other hand, is obtained through acid hydrolysis, resulting in rod-like crystalline cellulose nanoparticles. NCC is utilized in various fields such as electronics, aerospace, and biomedical engineering for its exceptional strength, stiffness, and optical properties.</p></p>
