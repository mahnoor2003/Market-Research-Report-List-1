<p><p>Carbon fiber technology is categorized into two market types: PAN (Polyacrylonitrile) and Pitch. PAN-based carbon fiber technology accounts for the majority share in the market. It involves the use of acrylonitrile as the raw material, which is then spun into fibers and processed further. PAN-based carbon fibers are widely used due to their superior mechanical properties, high strength, and excellent thermal and electrical conductivity. On the other hand, pitch-based carbon fiber technology utilizes petroleum or coal pitch as the raw material. Pitch-based carbon fibers offer enhanced chemical resistance and exceptional heat resistance properties.</p></p>
