<p><p>Corrosion-resistant alloys are categorized into three main types: iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based. Iron-based alloys, such as stainless steel, contain high amounts of iron along with other elements like chromium and nickel, providing excellent resistance to corrosion and oxidation. Nickel-based alloys, on the other hand, have a higher nickel content and offer exceptional resistance to a wide range of corrosive environments, including acids, alkalis, and seawater. Cobalt-based alloys, made primarily of cobalt and chromium, are resistant to corrosion, high temperatures, and wear, making them suitable for harsh environments like chemical plants and gas turbines.</p></p>
