Type I refers to cylinders that exert force in both directions, with hydraulic fluid acting on both sides of the piston. These cylinders are commonly used in construction, mining, and automotive industries. On the other hand, Type II cylinders are designed with a piston rod extending from both ends of the cylinder, allowing for greater flexibility in applications. Type II cylinders are commonly used in manufacturing, material handling, and industrial machinery industries.</p></p>
