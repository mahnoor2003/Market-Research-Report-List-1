<p><p>Electrophotographic printing is a versatile technology extensively used in the packaging industry. There are various types of electrophotographic printing methods available. Aqueous printing utilizes water-based ink that creates printed images through an evaporative drying process. Latex printing employs water-based latex inks, offering enhanced durability and environmental benefits. Solvent printing utilizes organic solvents to dissolve the ink pigments before drying. UV-cured printing utilizes ultraviolet light to rapidly cure the ink, resulting in instant drying and high-resolution images. These techniques cater to different packaging requirements, providing options for varying durability, speed, and environment friendliness.</p></p>
