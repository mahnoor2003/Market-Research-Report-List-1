<p><p>The embedded in-vehicle infotainment market includes various types of components. </p><p>1. Infotainment Unit: This refers to the central system that provides entertainment and information to the vehicle occupants. It typically includes a display screen for visual content, audio system for music, radio, and other media, as well as navigation features.</p><p>2. Control Panel: This component allows users to interact with the infotainment system. It includes buttons, knobs, and touch screens that enable users to control settings, select media options, or adjust audio settings.</p><p>3. Head-Up Display: This technology projects vital information like speed, navigation directions, and vehicle alerts onto the windshield, ensuring that drivers can access essential data without taking their eyes off the road.</p><p>4. Telematics Control Unit (TCU): This unit connects the vehicle to external networks, allowing for features such as remote diagnostics, emergency services, and vehicle tracking. It facilitates communication between the vehicle and external services through wireless connectivity.</p></p>
