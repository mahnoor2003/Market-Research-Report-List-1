<p><p>The Farm Animal External Medicine Market can be divided into two types: External Use and Internal Use. External Use medicine refers to medications that are applied directly to the external parts of the animal's body, such as sprays, creams, or ointments. These medicines are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, or parasites affecting the animal's external body. On the other hand, Internal Use medicine is administered internally to the animal, usually through injections or oral medications. These medicines are designed to treat internal infections, diseases, or imbalances within the animal's organs or systems.</p></p>
