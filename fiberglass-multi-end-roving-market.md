<p><p>The Fiberglass Multi End Roving market consists of two main types: Continuous Multi End Roving and Chopped Multi End Roving. Continuous Multi End Roving refers to a type of fiberglass roving that is typically used in applications where a continuous strand is needed, such as in filament winding or pultrusion processes. Chopped Multi End Roving, on the other hand, involves fiberglass strands that have been cut into shorter lengths, making them suitable for applications such as injection molding or compression molding where a shorter reinforcement is required. Overall, these two market types cater to different needs within the fiberglass industry.</p></p>
