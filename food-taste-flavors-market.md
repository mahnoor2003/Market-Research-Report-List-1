<p><p>The food taste flavors market comprises different types, including Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), Hydrolyzed Vegetable Protein (HVP), Yeast Extract, and others. MSG is a flavor enhancer derived from glutamic acid that adds a savory taste to food. HVP, obtained from plant sources, breaks down proteins into amino acids, providing a meaty taste. Yeast Extract, a byproduct of yeast fermentation, offers a rich umami flavor to food. The "Others" category encompasses various flavoring agents like disodium inosinate, disodium guanylate, and autolyzed yeast extract, each providing unique tastes to foods.</p></p>
