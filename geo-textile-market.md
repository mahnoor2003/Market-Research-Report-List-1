<p><p>Geo-textiles are materials used in civil engineering projects to enhance the performance and longevity of geotechnical structures. There are different types of geo-textiles available in the market. Nonwoven geo-textiles are made by bonding fibers together through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes. Woven geo-textiles are produced by weaving yarns together, providing strength and stability. Knitted geo-textiles are created by interlocking loops of yarn, offering flexibility and durability. Other types of geo-textiles include composite and hybrid options. Each type has unique characteristics suited for different applications, such as erosion control, soil stabilization, and drainage systems.</p></p>
