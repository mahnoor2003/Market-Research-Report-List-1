<p><p>Graphene-based supercapacitors are a type of energy storage device that utilize graphene as the main material. There are three main types: Electrical Double Layer Capacitors (EDLCs) store energy by the static charge separation at the interface of electrode and electrolyte; Pseudocapacitors store energy via fast redox reactions on the surface of the electrode material; Asymmetric supercapacitors combine both EDLC and pseudocapacitor mechanisms. These supercapacitors offer high energy density, fast charge/discharge rates, and long cycle life, making them ideal for various applications in electronics, transportation, and renewable energy sectors.</p></p>
