<p><p>High transmission glass refers to a type of glass that allows a significant amount of light to pass through without much loss. Plate glass refers to a flat, thick glass made by pouring molten glass onto a table and then allowing it to cool and solidify. Rolled glass, on the other hand, is a thin, flat glass produced by pouring molten glass onto a roller, which is then cooled and solidified. Both plate glass and rolled glass are commonly used in applications requiring high light transmission, such as windows, display panels, and optical devices.</p></p>
