<p><p>Impregnated graphite refers to graphite materials that have been infused or impregnated with different substances to enhance their properties for specific applications. There are two main types: resin impregnated and metal impregnated. Resin impregnated graphite involves the use of synthetic resins, such as phenolic or epoxy, which penetrate the graphite structure to improve its mechanical strength and resistance to chemicals. On the other hand, metal impregnated graphite utilizes metals like copper or silver to enhance its thermal and electrical conductivity while maintaining some graphite properties.</p></p>
