<p><p>Inorganic scintillators are crystalline materials that exhibit luminescence when exposed to ionizing radiation. These materials are extensively used in various applications such as healthcare, nuclear power plants, high-energy physics, and environmental monitoring. Inorganic scintillators offer advantages such as high density, high light yield, and good stopping power, making them suitable for detecting different types of radiation.</p><p>The global inorganic scintillators market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period. The growing prevalence of diseases such as cancer and the rising adoption of nuclear medicine for diagnostic purposes are driving the demand for inorganic scintillators in medical imaging applications.</p><p>Overall, the inorganic scintillators market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period, driven by technological advancements and increasing applications in various industries.</p></p>

<p><p>The inorganic scintillators market comprises various types, including alkali-halide crystals, oxide-based crystals, and others. Alkali-halide crystals are made of compounds like cesium iodide and sodium iodide, which emit light when struck by radiation. Similarly, oxide-based crystals such as yttrium aluminum garnet and cerium-doped gadolinium oxyorthosilicate produce light in response to radiation. The "others" category includes scintillators made of materials like bismuth germanate and lead tungstate. These scintillators find applications in medical imaging, homeland security, nuclear power plants, and high-energy physics experiments, to name a few.</p></p>
