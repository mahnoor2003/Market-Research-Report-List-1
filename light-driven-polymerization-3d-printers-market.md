<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Light-driven polymerization 3D printers use light as a source of energy to build three-dimensional structures. Top-down printers work by projecting light onto a resin surface, selectively solidifying it layer by layer. This process allows for high-resolution printing of intricate details. On the other hand, bottom-up printers start the printing process by exposing a liquid resin to a light source from the bottom. The solidification progresses in an upward direction, creating structures with good vertical accuracy. Both types provide unique benefits, but the choice depends on the desired application and the level of detail required.</p></p>
