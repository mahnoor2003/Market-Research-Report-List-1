<p><p>A Linux computer, also known as a Linux-based computer or a Linux system, refers to a computer system that runs on the Linux operating system (OS). The open-source nature of Linux has attracted a large community of developers and contributors, resulting in continuous innovation and improvement of the operating system. Linux-based computers have gained traction in various sectors, including education, government, enterprise, and IT services.</p><p>One of the key drivers of the Linux Computer Market is the increasing demand for secure and reliable operating systems. Linux is known for its robust security features, making it an attractive choice for organizations that prioritize data protection. These technologies enable the efficient utilization of hardware resources and enhance the scalability and performance of applications. Linux distributions specifically tailored for containers, such as Ubuntu Core and Red Hat Atomic Host, have emerged to cater to the growing demand in this area.</p><p>Furthermore, the Internet of Things (IoT) has provided new opportunities for Linux-based systems. Linux distributions designed for IoT devices, such as Debian, Yocto Project, and OpenWrt, have gained popularity due to their lightweight and customizable nature.
