<p><p>The locoregional hyperthermia system market includes two types of heat delivery systems: external and internal. The external heat delivery system involves the use of external devices, such as electromagnetic or ultrasound devices, to deliver heat directly to the affected area. On the other hand, the internal heat delivery system involves the insertion of a heat source inside the body, such as radiofrequency electrodes or microwave antennas, to directly heat the targeted region. Both systems are utilized in locoregional hyperthermia treatments to raise the temperature of tumors, enhancing the effectiveness of other therapies.</p></p>
