<p><p>Machine and factory automation refers to the use of technology and computer systems to control and monitor industrial processes. Continuous automation involves the continuous production of goods without stopping, using systems like conveyors and robots, enabling high volume and high-speed production. Intermittent automation, on the other hand, involves producing goods in batches, with breaks in between each production cycle. This type of automation is used when production quantities are smaller or when different products need to be manufactured within the same production line. Both types play a crucial role in increasing efficiency and productivity in manufacturing processes.</p></p>
