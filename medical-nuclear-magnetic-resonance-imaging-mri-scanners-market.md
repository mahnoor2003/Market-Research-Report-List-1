<p><p>Medical Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scanners are classified based on their field strength, which is measured in Tesla (T). Low field strength scanners typically range from 0.2T to 0.4T, mid field strength scanners range from 0.5T to 1.2T, and high field strength scanners range from 1.5T to 3.0T or higher. These classifications refer to the strength of the magnetic field generated by the scanner. Higher field strength scanners provide better image quality and resolution, but they are also more expensive and may have increased risks for certain patients.</p></p>
