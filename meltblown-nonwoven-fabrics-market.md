<p><p>Meltblown nonwoven fabrics are created through a specialized process known as melt blowing, where thermoplastic materials are melted and extruded to form fine fibers. These fibers are then rapidly cooled and collected to create a nonwoven fabric. With the rising concerns about air pollution and the need for effective filtration solutions, the demand for meltblown nonwoven fabrics for air and liquid filtration applications is on the rise. Additionally, the growing awareness about hygiene and the need for disposable products in medical and personal care sectors are further contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has significantly boosted the demand for meltblown nonwoven fabrics.
