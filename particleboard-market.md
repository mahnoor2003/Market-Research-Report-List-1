<p><p>Particleboard is a type of engineered wood product made from wood chips, sawmill shavings, and sawdust that are bonded together with a resin adhesive and compressed under high pressure and temperature. There are different market types for particleboard based on their specific applications and finishes. Standard chipboard refers to the basic form used in general construction. Melamine veneered chipboard has a thin layer of melamine applied for a decorative finish. Flooring grade chipboard is specifically designed for flooring applications. Wood veneered chipboard and plastic veneered chipboard have a thin layer of wood or plastic applied for aesthetic purposes. Finally, there are other specialized markets for particleboard such as fire-retardant or moisture-resistant variants.</p></p>
