<p><p>Photoplethysmographs (PPGs) are medical devices used to measure changes in blood volume and oxygen saturation in tissues. Single-channel PPGs use one sensor to detect light absorption and reflection, providing basic readings. On the other hand, double-channel PPGs utilize additional sensors to capture more comprehensive data, including arterial stiffness and blood pressure. Single-channel devices are cost-effective and widely used for basic monitoring purposes. In contrast, double-channel devices offer more advanced measurements and are typically employed in clinical settings or research studies requiring detailed analysis.</p></p>
