<p><p>The pickup truck aluminum alloy wheel market can be classified into various types such as casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into a wheel mold and allowing it to cool and solidify. This method is cost-effective and allows for intricate designs. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated aluminum by applying pressure, creating a stronger and more durable wheel. Lastly, the "other" category includes alternative methods, such as flow forming or hybrid processes, which offer unique characteristics such as lightweight or added strength to cater to different market demands.</p></p>
