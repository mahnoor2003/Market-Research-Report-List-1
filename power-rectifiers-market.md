<p><p>Power rectifiers are electronic devices used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC). The market for power rectifiers is categorized into two main types: diode rectifiers and thyristor rectifiers. Diode rectifiers use diodes to convert AC to DC by allowing current flow in one direction only, making them suitable for low power applications. Thyristor rectifiers, on the other hand, utilize Thyristor technology to control power flow with higher efficiency, making them ideal for high power applications. Both types play a crucial role in various industries, including power transmission, automotive, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
