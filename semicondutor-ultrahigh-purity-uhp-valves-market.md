<p><p>The semiconductor ultrahigh purity (UHP) valves market can be categorized into two types: Low Flow and High Flow. </p><p>Low Flow valves are designed to control the flow of gases or fluids at a relatively low rate, typically used in applications that require precision and accuracy. They are suited for delicate processes in semiconductors where small amounts of materials need to be delivered or isolated.</p><p>On the other hand, High Flow valves are used to regulate the flow of gases or fluids at a much higher rate, suitable for larger-scale operations. They are commonly employed in systems that demand rapid delivery or extraction of materials, such as during wafer fabrication in semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
