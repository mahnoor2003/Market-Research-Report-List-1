<p><p>Silicon carbide continuous fibers are used in various industries for their exceptional properties. The market types for these fibers include PreCeramic Polymer Pyrolysis (3P), Chemical Vapor Deposited (CVD), Chemical Vapor Reaction (CVR), and other markets. </p><p>In PreCeramic Polymer Pyrolysis (3P), silicon carbide fibers are produced by heating preceramic polymers to high temperatures, resulting in the formation of SiC. Chemical Vapor Deposited (CVD) method involves the deposition of silicon and carbon compounds onto a substrate, which then reacts to form SiC fibers. Chemical Vapor Reaction (CVR) utilizes gaseous reactants that directly react to form the fibers. Various other methods also exist for producing silicon carbide continuous fibers.</p></p>
