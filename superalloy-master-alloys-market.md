<p><p>Superalloy master alloys are categorized into three types based on their main component: iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based. Iron-based master alloys primarily consist of iron with other elements like chromium and aluminium, offering excellent corrosion resistance and high temperature strength. Nickel-based master alloys comprise nickel as the primary element with added components like chromium and molybdenum, providing high creep and oxidation resistance. Cobalt-based master alloys mainly contain cobalt with additives such as chromium and tungsten, resulting in exceptional wear and corrosion resistance, along with high strength at elevated temperatures.</p></p>
