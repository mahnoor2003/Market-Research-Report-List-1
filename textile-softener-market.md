Textile softeners can be categorized into three types: cationic, anionic, and non-ionic based on their chemical composition.</p><p>The global textile softener market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include increasing disposable income, changing consumer preferences towards luxury and comfortable clothing, and the growing demand for high-quality fabrics in the fashion and apparel industry. <p><p>Textile softeners are available in various types to cater to different consumer preferences. The liquid type is a commonly used softener that can be added directly to the wash cycle. Dryer sheets are convenient thin sheets that can be placed in the dryer to soften fabrics. Dryer balls, on the other hand, are solid balls made of materials like wool or plastic that are also added to the dryer to achieve softness. Each type of softener offers a convenient and effective way to make clothes, towels, and other textiles feel softer and more comfortable.</p></p>
