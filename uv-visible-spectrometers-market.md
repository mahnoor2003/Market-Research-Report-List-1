<p><p>UV-Visible spectrometers refer to devices used to measure the absorption and transmission of light in the UV and visible range of the electromagnetic spectrum. Single-beam UV-Vis spectrophotometers are instruments that utilize light from a single light source, while double-beam UV-Vis spectrophotometers use two light sources. The main difference between the two is that single-beam spectrophotometers can only measure one sample at a time, while double-beam spectrophotometers can simultaneously measure a sample and a reference, allowing for more accurate measurements and better stability.</p></p>
